Sugar is a quick source of energy for the body. It's broken down into glucose, which is then used by cells to produce energy.
Sugar releases serotonin, a neurotransmitter that helps improve mood and reduce stress.
Sugar is used as a preservative to extend the shelf life of foods like jams, jellies, and canned goods.
Consuming too much sugar can lead to weight gain and obesity, as it's high in calories and low in nutrients.
Excessive sugar consumption has been linked to an increased risk of chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and certain cancers.
Sugar is a primary source of energy for bacteria in the mouth, which can lead to tooth decay and cavities.
Sugar can be addictive, and overconsumption can lead to cravings and withdrawal symptoms.
A diet high in sugar can disrupt the balance of gut bacteria, leading to digestive issues and other health problems.
